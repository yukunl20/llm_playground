Summarizing text is one of the most exciting applications of large language models. It allows you to process volumes of text that would be impossible for a human to read in detail, such as summarizing hundreds of product reviews for a dashboard to quickly understand customer sentiment.

#### Technical Setup

The following helper function is used to interact with the model programmatically.

```python
import openai

def get_completion(prompt, model="gpt-3.5-turbo"):
    messages = [{"role": "user", "content": prompt}]
    response = openai.ChatCompletion.create(
        model=model,
        messages=messages,
        temperature=0,
    )
    return response.choices.message["content"]
```

-------------
#### Basic Summarization

To summarize text, you provide the content and a specific constraint, such as a word limit, character count, or number of sentences.

```python
review = "..." # Product review text here

prompt = f"""
Your task is to generate a short summary of a product review \
from an e-commerce website. 

Summarize the review below, delimited by triple 
backticks, in at most 30 words. 

Review: ```{review}```
"""
response = get_completion(prompt)
```

#### Summarizing with a Specific Focus

You can tailor a summary for a specific department within a business by instructing the model to focus on particular aspects of the text.

- **Shipping Focus:** Useful for feedback on delivery times and packaging.
- **Pricing Focus:** Useful for feedback on perceived value and cost.

```python
prompt = f"""
Your task is to generate a short summary of a product review \
from an e-commerce website to give feedback to the shipping department. 

Summarize the review below, delimited by triple 
backticks, in at most 30 words, and focus on any aspects 
that mention shipping and delivery of the product. 

Review: ```{review}```
"""
```

---
#### Summarizing vs. Extracting

While a focused summary might still include general information, you can use the **"extract"** command if you want the model to provide _only_ the specific information requested, omitting all other context.

```python
prompt = f"""
Your task is to extract relevant information from a product \
review to give feedback to the shipping department. 

From the review below, delimited by triple backticks, \
extract the information related to shipping and delivery. \
Limit to 30 words. 

Review: ```{review}```
"""
```

#### Bulk Summarization Workflow

To handle a large volume of data, you can store reviews in a list and loop through them to generate a series of concise summaries. This is ideal for building a high-level overview or dashboard.

```python
reviews = [review_1, review_2, review_3, review_4]

for i in range(len(reviews)):
    prompt = f"""
    Your task is to generate a short summary of a product \
    review from an e-commerce website. 

    Summarize the review below, delimited by triple \
    backticks in at most 20 words. 

    Review: ```{reviews[i]}```
    """
    response = get_completion(prompt)
    print(i, response, "\n")
```
